Since its founding in 1827, KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm has grown to become one of Europe’s leading technical and engineering universities, as well as a key centre of intellectual talent and innovation. We are Sweden’s largest technical research and learning institution and home to students, researchers and faculty from around the world dedicated to advancing knowledge.

Website: www.kth.se

Who can contribute to github.com/KTH?

New repositories

Any employee at KTH can publish software or eqvivalent on github.com/KTH after getting authorised to do so by the KTH IT-department. Every repository also has to be documented and authorised by submitting a request the IT-department at KTH, prior to publishing.

Pull requests

We normally work with, and accept pull request, but we can not promise to accept or look at them. But we can promise to do our best with the time we have.

Open source license

The default license for open source distributed for github.com/KTH is MIT license. Some repositories may have other licenses.

If you find a repository in github.com/KTH that is missing a LICENCE-file, please contact us.

Contact

Github.com/KTH is maintained by IT-department at KTH. You can contact us on infosys@kth.se.

Legacy repositories

Github.com/KTHse is an old Github organisation for KTH. No new repositories will be published there in the future.
